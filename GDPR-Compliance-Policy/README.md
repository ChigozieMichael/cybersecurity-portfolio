# GDPR Compliance Policy Development Project 

## Overview
This project demonstrates the development of a comprehensive GDPR Compliance Policy for a fictional organization, DELAP WARES INC. The policy aligns with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and establishes a robust governance framework for data protection, security controls, and legal compliance. The work reflects practical cybersecurity governance and GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance) competencies.

## Objective
To create an enterprise-level GDPR Compliance Policy that:
- Ensures lawful, fair, and transparent processing of personal data.
- Establishes organizational and technical safeguards for data protection.
- Defines employee responsibilities, third-party oversight, and breach handling.
- Supports regulatory compliance and risk reduction for the organization.

## Scope of Policy
The policy applies to:
- All employees: HR, Customer Support, IT, Legal, Marketing, Operations, Finance, Logistics, and Security.
- Contractors and consultants.
- Third-party vendors and data processors (e.g., PAYDAY TECH).

Coverage includes:
- Data protection principles  
- Data subject rights  
- Data security requirements  
- Breach notification workflows  
- Vendor management  
- Employee responsibilities  
- Policy enforcement and annual review

## Key Sections Developed

### 1. Data Protection Principles  
Documented and applied GDPR Article 5 principles:
- Lawfulness, fairness, transparency  
- Purpose limitation  
- Data minimization  
- Accuracy  
- Storage limitation  
- Integrity and confidentiality  

### 2. Data Subject Rights  
Designed processes enabling rights such as:
- Access requests (DSARs)  
- Right to erasure  
- Data portability  
- Rectification  
Compliance with Articles 15–20.

### 3. Data Security Measures  
Defined technical and organizational controls, including:
- Encryption at rest and in transit  
- MFA and access control policies  
- Vulnerability assessments and penetration testing  
- Backup and recovery processes  
- Business continuity controls  

Aligned with Article 32.

### 4. Incident Response and Data Breach Notification  
Created breach management workflows:
- Detection and assessment  
- Notification to authorities within 72 hours  
- Communication to affected individuals  
- Breach documentation obligations  

Aligned with Articles 33 and 34.

### 5. Third-Party Data Processors  
Outlined vendor governance:
- Mandatory GDPR-compliant Data Processing Agreements  
- Subprocessor oversight  
- Contractual obligations for compliance  
Aligned with Article 28.

### 6. Employee Responsibilities  
Developed internal rules requiring:
- Mandatory GDPR training  
- Role-specific data handling practices  
- Prohibition of unauthorized data access or sharing  

### 7. Policy Governance  
- Annual review cycles  
- Enforcement mechanisms  
- Executive approval structure
